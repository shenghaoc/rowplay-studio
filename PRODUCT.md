# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Dedicated Concept2 erg athletes (RowErg, SkiErg, BikeErg) reviewing their own training data post-session. They sit down at their Mac after a workout to analyze performance, spot trends, replay sessions, and plan their next piece. The context is deep analysis and personal reflection — not casual glance metrics or social feed scrolling. They know their numbers and expect precision.

## Product Purpose

RowPlay Studio is a native macOS port of rowplay — a Concept2 logbook analytics and real-time workout replay app. It exists to give erg athletes a fast, offline-capable, privacy-respecting tool for understanding their training data without the noise of social platforms. Success means the athlete closes the app with a clearer picture of their performance than when they opened it.

## Brand Personality

- **Clean** — no clutter, no unnecessary chrome, every element earns its place
- **Energetic** — color accents and motion convey the physicality of the sport without gamification
- **Focused** — deep analysis tools for the dedicated athlete, not a casual fitness dashboard

## Anti-references

- **Strava / Garmin Connect** — avoid dark/black fitness-app aesthetic, over-gamification (badges, challenges, leaderboards as primary UX), social feed clutter, and the generic "fitness dashboard" visual language
- Avoid gratuitous card grids with identical icon + heading + text patterns
- No hero-metric templates (big number, small label, supporting stats, gradient accent)
- No glassmorphism, gradient text, or decorative blurs

## Design Principles

1. **Data clarity over decoration** — Metrics come first. Chrome, color, and typography serve the numbers, not the other way around. If removing an element doesn't hurt comprehension, remove it.
2. **Earn the athlete's trust** — Precision in every number shown. No exaggerated claims, no rounding that misleads. The athlete knows their splits to the tenth of a second; the app must match that standard.
3. **Focused depth** — Every screen has one clear purpose. The dashboard scans; the detail view analyzes; the replay relives. Don't blur boundaries between modes.
4. **Quiet energy** — Energetic through deliberate color accents and purposeful motion, not through noise, badges, or gamification. The energy of the sport is felt, not shouted.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target WCAG 2.1 AA-equivalent contrast and semantics where they apply to native macOS UI
- Support reduced motion preferences (already implemented in replay engine)
- Color is never the sole differentiator — pair semantic colors with labels, icons, or position
- Accessible labels and values on all metric tiles and interactive elements
